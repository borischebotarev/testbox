Understanding Your
Interview Report

Your interview performance is analyzed and measured across four key dimensions: Confidence, Clarity, Convincing, and Creativity. Learn more below how we measure each category, and how to improve for the future.

Confidence

This is a measure of how well you are perceived to know the subject matter you are talking about. To achieve a high score in confidence, you must learn to be fluent, ideate, and ultimately influence. Use the following additional tips to assist you in achieving this goal:

  • Speaking Clearly: Speak clearly and fluidly. Maintain a smooth and steady flow of speech without frequent hesitations, interruptions, or pauses.
  • Appropriate Volume + Tone: Use an appropriate volume and tone of voice. Moderate your tone to convey enthusiasm, authority, or empathy as needed.
  • Effective Pauses: Be smart about using pauses strategically. These pauses should be used for emphasis, and to allow the listener to process information.
  • Topic Mastery: Demonstrate a command of a particular topic by sharing thoughtful examples and insights.
  • Learning Orientation: Share in ways that communicate you’re a learner who seeks advice, and who wants to improve at work and in life.

Clarity

This is a measure of how well you may be understood by other listeners. To achieve a high score in clarity, speak with a steady pace, that is not too fast, or too slow to understand. Use the following additional tips to assist you in achieving this goal:

  • Conciseness: Communicate in ways that are as straightforward as possible. Be direct in answering the questions asked, providing specific examples when and where appropriate.
  • Organized Structure: Do your best to present your ideas and stories in a well-structured manner, making it easier for listeners to follow along. To accomplish this, think about the following framework: Situation-Action-Result-Learning.
  • Clear & Direct Language: Avoid using strange, unusual words and/or phrases that might confuse the interviewer - including some language you mostly use only with friends.
  • Avoid Jargon: Try not to use special words or phrases that might be difficult for the interviewer to understand, including jargon, slang or abbreviations.

Convincing

This is a measure of how likely you are to influence your listener with your thoughts and speech. To achieve a high score in influence, attempt to reduce the use of words and phrases that indicate uncertainty. Avoid speculation or guessing. Use the following additional tips to assist you in achieving this goal:

  • Show Expertise: Display as much knowledge or expertise in your responses as possible. Research the company, as well as the type of role for which you’re interviewing.
  • Use Reliable Sources: Use reliable sources of information and references, such as Google searches regarding best practices in customer service jobs.
  • Be Transparent & Honest: Be honest and open in your communication. Do not exaggerate information so that you come across as being someone other than who you are. If you don’t know the answer it’s okay to say so, and to note that you’re eager to learn!

Creativity

This is a measure of how many concepts or ideas you are able to communicate effectively while speaking. To achieve a high score in ideation, attempt to describe ideas in new ways with novel vocabulary. Avoid repetitions. Use the following additional tips to assist you in achieving this goal:

  • Creative Problem-Solving: Approach problems creatively. Propose unique solutions others might not have considered.
  • Connect Diverse Concepts: Connect seemingly unrelated ideas or concepts to form new and meaningful relationships.
  • Offer Unique Perspectives: Offer different perspectives on familiar topics. Let your authenticity - and who you are - shine.
